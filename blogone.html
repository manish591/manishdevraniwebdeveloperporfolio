Design Resource Guide From A Programmer

Hi, I'm a programmer, and not a designer. Over the years I've been building websites, a lot of my clients ask about how to improve their design. That's why I decided to write this blog post. In my opinion, the details can make or break a website. However, most designers are usually not interested in the fine details. That's where I come in. As you can see from the title this article is targeted at programmers/developers who are trying to improve their website's design without hiring a designer.

If you are a developer looking for inspirational design resources or simply want to expand your knowledge about design, then you are on the right place. My collection includes some of the most useful and best-designed resources I stumbled upon in my marathons through the Internet.These design resources will definitely aid you in your projects. Color Pallete Resources

Finding the right color for your website can be hard. Then, you asked 10 different people to tell you the best choice. Out of those 10 different people, 3 or 4 gave you the same advice. Now what do you do? Do you listen to them? Which websites should I use? What about books and tools? That is why we created this blog post - to provide a place where you can find answers to all of these questions. In this article, we will talk about some great resources and tools that will help with choosing the right colors.

Adobe Color

Adobe Color is a web-based color palette generator. Its easy-to-use interface lets you generate beautiful, harmonious color palettes for projects like wedding planning and interior design, as well as for whatever else your creative heart desires. It has quiet nice features, It allows you to generate your custom color pallete, and you can save it for later. You can also follow trends in color pallete.

Coolors

Coolors.co is a color palette generator, built upon the foundations of simplicity and ease-of-use. Here you'll be able to generate color combinations in the form of both palettes and schemes. The Color Wheel allows users to choose a base color and manually adjust the hue, or automatically adjust the hue from an image. Coolors also offers a Schemes section that lets you create custom color schemes directly from any image on the web.

Stock Images Resources

Another important aspect in web design is the images used. Good (or bad) images can make a wonderful website look great or terrible. I use stock images to increase the beauty of my work, but I don’t know any good site where you can download free stock images. Also, most of the sites are really tricky and force you to register just to download one image. That’s why I have decided to make this post about best stock image sources.

Pexels

pexels is great stock image website which provide you with high quality images for free. More than 8000+ photos in their library and they’re adding new photos every day. The key element of taking a photo from online is licenece. With pexels you don't have to worry about licencing freely use these in your design.

Freepik

freepik is truly a great place to obtain free images that are of higher quality. I am so glad that I found out about this amazing platform. As someone who has done various types of online business, I find it very important to have access to great and high-quality images that can be used for web purposes and will not cost you a fortune. freepik can offer you both premium and free images. It also offers you icons, illustration , mockups etc.

Illustration Resources

There are lots of resources when it comes to illustrations. You can get one from stock photo websites. However, these don’t always come with high quality. Therefore, you need someone who can create a unique illustration that can work for your website. These illustrations also depend on what niche you are in and the kind of presentation you want for your product or service. If you need help, check out some of these illustration resources and find out more about the kind of illustration resources you can use for your online business.

UnDraw

undraw is a social network for illustrators and their illustration fans. This is a very cool illustration website that you should know about. undraw is a great place to find creative inspiration. There are many professional illustrators contributing their work to this website and you can use them in your works without any credit. One of the cool feature of this website is that you can get illustration in color you selected.

DrawKit

Drawkit is freemium based illustration website that offer various design illustrations to you. good for portfolio websites. Drawkit offers a collection of free, paid and customizable vector illustrations, ideal for adapting and adding to any design. You must check this out if you are serious about improving your design skills.

Icons Resources

according to my experience, finding icons on the internet is a difficult task. icon search engines don’t always work and finding icons on google is an impossible task. i put together this list of resources that can help you find those elusive icons that you need for your website. You can edit icons with custom colors in these websites, They work on freemium model so you have to pay for extra features.

Flaticon

flaticon.com is a great website for getting custamizable free icons. They are of highest quality and thats why all of our clients who want to make a professional website use them. Some of the best free vector art image suite we have tested. The Flaticon platform has 2 million icons and counting—all for free with required attribution.

Iconscout

this section is basically on free iconwebsite named iconoscout, which has some amazing quality icons. they are 100% free. they are very colorful and beautifully designed. it would be a great addition for your site or design project. You can also customize them and save for later.
